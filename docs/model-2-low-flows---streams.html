8.2 Model 2: LOW FLOWS - streams

For this model demonstration, our EEMs dataset has been filtered to ‚Äòstream‚Äô samples only (no wells) (n= 91)

ABOUT THIS MODEL - stream samples during low flows (August 1 - April 30) n=58 - response is HUMIC.

Our indicators for each latent variable are as follows:
Topo:
‚Äìmean distance that allochthonous water has to travel to the stream channel
‚Äìmean aspect in radians
‚Äìmean subcatchment TWI
Runoff
‚Äì log_discharge_m2
Landcover:
‚Äì % of subcatchment pixels with beetle kill and harvested/regenerating vegetation Nutrient: ‚Äì nitrate [NO3]mg/L in water sample Usually the dominant form of dissolved N exported from forests via streams(Ryan and Lawrence, 2024). stimulates microbial activity, potentially reducing the fraction on labile carbon in the water column. Microbes can polymerize or oxydize simpler carbon molecules producing humic-like substances that are more recalcitrant (T.C. Balser 2005). HUMIC:
‚Äì ‚ÄúPARAF_comp_1‚Äù = total loading of humic component 1. A ‚Äòtotal loading‚Äô is a number that tells us how much of a particular compoent is present in a particular sample.
‚Äì ‚ÄúPARAF_comp_2‚Äù = total loading of humic component 2.
‚Äì ‚Äúrelative_load_comp_1‚Äù, = the fraction of the total sample absorbance by component 1
‚Äì ‚Äúrelative_load_comp_2‚Äù, = the fraction of the total sample absorbance by component 2
‚Äì ‚ÄúFluorescence_Index‚Äù = fluorescence index (FI) as the ratio of emission intensities at ex=370 em=470: ex370 em520 (cory et al.¬†2010)
‚Äì ‚ÄúSUVA254‚Äù = UV absorbance at 254 nm (SUVA254) normalized by DOC concentration: reflects the aromaticity level of DOM
